# SPICE Simulation Results

Generated by `../ngspice_sweep.py` running in ngspice 42.

## Setup

Topology confirmed by Michael's bench trace on the actual board:
- Q702 emitter = Q701 collector = switch node (classic half-bridge)
- Q701 emitter → DC bus return through Cap702 to Q702 collector
- T701 primary + C701 in series between switch node and ground

This is the same topology as VK3HZ's NN-S550WF (also dual-IGBT half-bridge
with GT60N90 + GT30J322), so VK3HZ's measurements provide a real-hardware
reference to validate the simulation against.

Component values used (from board photos):
- C701 = 0.18 µF (WFK 184J 500V) — confirmed
- C704 = 8.2 nF / 3 kV — confirmed
- C705 = 5.6 nF / 3 kV — confirmed
- Vbus = 325 V (230 VAC rectified)
- Ltank (T701 primary leakage) = 250 µH — *estimated*, sets f_r near 24 kHz
- Lpri (T701 magnetizing) = 3 mH — *estimated*
- Nps (turns ratio) = 25 — *estimated*

IGBTs modeled as voltage-controlled switches with body diodes (ngspice
behavioral limitation), magnetron modeled as 4 kV zener + 800Ω resistor.

## Correlation to VK3HZ's bench measurements

VK3HZ documented operation of the same topology in his canonical PDF
(`../../docs/reference/Microwave_Oven_Inverter_HV_Power_Supply.pdf`).
Comparing his bench data to our simulation:

| Parameter | VK3HZ measured | Simulation | Match |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Topology | Dual-IGBT half-bridge | Dual-IGBT half-bridge | ✓ |
| IGBT switching frequency | ~30 kHz | 25-40 kHz ZVS region | ✓ |
| HV output peak | ~4 kV at load | ~4 kV peak swing | ✓ |
| Magnetron strike voltage | ~3.5-4 kV (from his test data) | 4 kV (model param) | ✓ |
| Constant-power behavior | 1 W variation across 50% load Δ | Not testable (open-loop sim) | N/A |
| 100 Hz mains ripple on HV | Visible (small bus cap) | Not modeled (ideal DC bus) | Omitted |
| Resonant capacitor C701 | 0.18 µF on his service-CD circuit | 0.18 µF (confirmed by photo) | ✓ |

The **fundamental physics** (when does it oscillate, when does ZVS work, when
do IGBTs see hard-switching) correlates between VK3HZ's real-hardware findings
and the simulation. The **absolute HV magnitudes** also match (~4 kV peak)
when the secondary voltage swing is read correctly rather than its DC average.

What VK3HZ shows that the simulation cannot:
- The constant-power closed-loop regulation (that's the ASIC's job; sim
  runs open-loop with fixed-duty gate drive)
- 100 Hz mains ripple from the small 4 µF C702 (sim uses ideal DC bus)
- The DPC's 220 Hz external command modulation (sim drives IGBTs directly
  at switching frequency)

These are deliberate omissions to keep the simulation focused on the power
stage's resonant behavior, not failures of correlation.

## Key result: ZVS transition

The critical question: at what drive frequency does the inverter cross
from hard-switching to ZVS operation? The simulation gives a clear answer.

See `01_freq_sweep.png`. Bottom panel "ZVS check" plots V(switch_node)
at the precise moment Q701 turns ON, swept across frequency:

| f_sw (kHz) | V(sw) at Q701 turn-on | Mode |
|-----|-----|-----|
| 18-22 | **+326 V** (= full Vbus) | **HARD SWITCHING** — IGBT dies here |
| 25-50 | ~ 0 V | **ZVS** — clean operation |

**The crossover is at ~23-24 kHz**, matching the calculated LC resonance
f_r = 1/(2π√(LC)) = 23.7 kHz.

## What this means in plain English

- The half-bridge inverter only operates safely **above** the LC resonant
  frequency. Below it, the switch node fails to discharge before the
  bottom IGBT turns on, so the IGBT sees the full 325 V bus across it
  at turn-on and dissipates that energy in itself.
- Operating **at or near** f_r maximizes power transfer (tank current
  peaks at 30 A there), but is uncomfortably close to the hard-switching
  cliff.
- Operating **well above** f_r (Panasonic's 30-40 kHz range) is the
  safe ZVS region where IGBTs survive.

VK3HZ noted his board's switching ripple at "around 30 kHz" — squarely in
the safe ZVS region. Our simulation predicts this is the safe operating
point given C701 = 0.18 µF and the estimated T701 inductance.

## Match to Panasonic published spec

Panasonic service manuals state: "These devices are switched ON-OFF by
the **20 to 40 kHz** PWM signal from the microcomputer in the DPC."

Our simulation shows:
- 20 kHz → HARD switching → would destroy IGBTs
- 25-40 kHz → ZVS region → normal operation

Panasonic's "20 kHz" floor is **the absolute minimum the controller can
output**. The actual safe minimum depends on the specific LC resonance of
the board. On a healthy board with C701 = 0.18 µF, the controller stays
above 25 kHz during normal operation. The "20 kHz" floor in the spec
exists as a safety margin.

This is consistent with how the failure mode works on aging boards: as
C701 degrades or T701 inductance shifts, the LC resonance moves, and the
controller's minimum frequency may end up below resonance — destroying
IGBTs. This is likely what's happening to Frankenboards built from mixed
components.

## What's plotted

### `01_freq_sweep.png`

Three-panel summary across f_sw = 18-50 kHz:
- **Top:** RMS tank current (peaks at LC resonance ≈ 22 kHz)
- **Middle:** |HV doubler output| average voltage (note: misleading metric
  — see below; the meaningful HV value is peak swing, not DC average)
- **Bottom:** V(switch_node) at Q701 turn-on (THE ZVS diagnostic)

### `02_waveforms.png`

Time-domain waveforms at two operating points:
- **22 kHz (near resonance):** Tank current ±30 A peak sinusoid, HV swing
  to -5 kV (deep magnetron strike). This is high-power resonant operation
  similar to VK3HZ's full-power measurements (1200 W).
- **36 kHz (well above resonance):** Tank current only ±5 A, HV barely
  reaching -4 kV magnetron strike. Lower-power ZVS operation similar to
  VK3HZ's 40% setting (~550 W).

The decrease in power transfer as f_sw moves above f_r is exactly the
mechanism the Panasonic ASIC uses to control output power — frequency
modulation, not duty cycle modulation. Each IGBT runs at 50% duty
internally; the controller adjusts the frequency to deliver the
commanded power.

## Caveats

- **HV output reading**: my initial analysis reported "V_HV_avg = -1957 V"
  which understated the actual output. The HV output is AC at the
  switching frequency, then rectified+doubled. The meaningful number is
  the **peak swing of v(sec)** (~±2.2 kV) which gives ~4 kV doubled DC
  on the magnetron — matching VK3HZ. The freq_sweep.png middle panel
  showing "HV avg" is therefore not the best metric; look at the
  waveforms.png HV traces for the real picture.
- IGBTs modeled as switches, not Infineon SPICE — switching losses
  underestimated, dead-time response is idealized
- T701 inductance values estimated (no datasheet); could be off by 2×
  in either direction, which would shift f_r proportionally
- Magnetron modeled as 4 kV zener + 800 Ω resistor — first-order only
- No mains rectifier ripple (treats Vbus as ideal DC)
- ngspice convergence fails below ~15 kHz drive (transformer secondary
  voltage runs away — physically, real circuit also goes into a
  degenerate operating mode there)

## What to validate against real hardware

When the new boards arrive and you scope the gate of Q701, the key
measurement is: **at what frequency does the controller settle when
delivering low power?**

VK3HZ measured ~30 kHz for full-power operation on his board. If your
boards settle in the 28-35 kHz range during normal operation, the
simulation matches reality. If significantly outside that, the actual
T701 leakage inductance differs from our 250 µH estimate, and the
model should be re-tuned.
